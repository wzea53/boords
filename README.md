## What Is Boords, Exactly?

Boords is a browser-based storyboarding platform for video production teams. You create storyboards in it, share them with clients via a simple link, collect frame-by-frame feedback, track approval status, and export everything when you're done.

No PowerPoint. No PDF shuffling. No *"wait, which version are we on?"*

It's been around since 2015, was built by the co-founders of [Animade](https://animade.tv) — a well-regarded London animation studio — and is used by teams at Framestore, Partizan, Digital Brew, and a whole lot of smaller agencies who just want the pre-production process to not be a complete disaster.

## The Features That Actually Matter

### Collaborative Storyboards With Client Review Links

The core thing Boords does really well is the client review flow. You make your boards, hit share, send a link, and the client can leave comments **on specific frames** — not in a separate email thread, not in a Slack channel, not on a Post-it note that gets lost. Right on the frame.

Clients don't need to create an account. They just click the link and start commenting. That alone has probably saved thousands of creative teams from the slow death-by-email feedback loop.

You can also password-protect the link, track approval status per frame, and always know exactly who has and hasn't signed off.

### AI Image Generation

This is newer, but it's good. Boords has an AI image generator built right into the storyboard editor. You can describe a scene in plain language — "woman at kitchen sink, morning light, filling a water bottle" — and get a storyboard frame back in seconds.

There's also a **Character Guidelines** feature that tries to keep characters visually consistent across frames, which is genuinely one of the harder problems with AI-generated storyboards. It doesn't always nail it perfectly, but it's miles better than trying to prompt your way to consistency manually.

The Standard plan comes with 1,000 AI images per month. The Workflow plan gives you unlimited.

### Script Sync and Shot Lists

You can write (or paste) your script directly in Boords, right alongside your frames. The script and the storyboard panels stay in sync as you work. It also auto-generates a shot list from your storyboard — camera angle, shot type, notes — all structured and ready to take to set.

For anyone who's ever maintained a storyboard in one tab and a shot list in another and quietly suffered, this is a relief.

### Animatics

Drop your frames onto a timeline, set timing, add audio, and you've got an animatic — a rough animated preview of your storyboard that you can share with clients or use internally to check pacing before committing to a full cut.

Export it as an MP4. Done. No After Effects required.

### Version History

Every revision is tracked. You can see what changed between drafts and share specific versions with clients. No more mystery about which version got approved.
